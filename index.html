LIFE AND CAREER

Kofi Atta Annan, known worldwide as the seventh UN Secretary General was a Ghanaian born on the 8th of April 1983 as a twin with a twin sister called Efua Attaa in Kumasi, the capital of Ashanti Region in Ghana

He attended Mfantsipim Methodist School in Cape Coast from 1954 to 1957 in which he graduated. On the very same year he graduated, Ghana then called Gold Coast gained independence from the British colonization.

He then in 1958 studied ecomonics in NKUST in Kumasi where he recieved a Ford Foundation grant, which enabled him to complete his undergraduate studies in at Macalester College in St. Paul Minnesota, USA in 1961.

then completed a diplôme d'études approfondies DEA degree in International Relations at The Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies in Geneva, Switzerland, from 1961–62. After some years of work experience, he studied at the MIT Sloan School of Management (1971–72) in the Sloan Fellows program and earned a master's degree in management.

Kofi Annan was a father of 3 children and got married twice with his first marriage being with Titi Alakija lasting for 18yrs (1965 - 1983) and the second with Nane Lagergren which lasted till his passing.

Kofi Annan joined the UN in 1962, working for the WHO's Geneva office. He went on to work at the UN headquarter in several capacities including serving as the Under Secretary General for peacekeeping from march 1992 to december 1996 by the General Assembly making him the first office holder to be elected from the UN staff.

He held the position of the UN Secretary General for 2 terms (1997-2001, 2002-2006) during which he reformed the UN bereaucracy and got awarded the Noble Peace Prize which was then divided between him and UN by the Noble Commitee. His worked to combat HIV, especially in Africa also got recognition by the Noble Committee and additionally launched the UN Global Compact. After the end of his term, he founded the Kofi Annan Foundation in 2007 to work on international development.

DEATH AND STATE FUNERAL

Kofi Annan died on the morning of 18th of August 2018 in Bern, Switzerland, at the age of 80 after a very short illness.On the September 10, his body was returned to the native family in Ghana accompanied by his widow Nane, his children and senior diplomats from the international organsation where a brief and solemn ceremony was held at the Kotoka International Airport in Accra.

A state funeral was held for Kofi Annan on the 13th September 2018 coupled with other major activities in his honour.
